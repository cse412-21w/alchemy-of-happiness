Alchemy of Happiness

Everyone wants to be happy and all countries want their citizens to be happy.

However, the question is what makes our citizens happy? What are the most important factors that makes our people happy?

To study this, our group has decided to dig a little bit deeper into global happiness and find out what are the leading factors behind these happiness scores.

Our data set comes from World Happiness Report. It is a survey of the global happiness and gain recognitions from governments, organizations and civil societies.

The reports review the state of happiness in the world today and show how the new science of happiness explains personal and national variations in happiness.

To achieve our goal, we selected 4 potential factors in the report, trust in government, economy, life expectancy, and GDP per capita. We are hoping to see how does each factor effect the happiness score? Which factor is the leading cause to a country's happiness.

Quick Overview of Happiness Score and Life Expectancy:

Darker regions under happiness score corresponds with darker shaded dots under life expectancy factor.

The scatter plot illustrates the relationship between the two variables, showing all five years of data with X-axis being Happiness Score and Y-axis being Life Expectancy.

We divided the points into four sub-sections by high/low life expectancy & high/low happiness score. As we can see that majority of the data is divided into Quadrant I (+,+) and Quadrant III (-,-), which means life expectancy directly relates to its happiness score.

More specificly, high life expectancy countries has high happiness score and, conversely, low life expectancy countries has low happiness score.
